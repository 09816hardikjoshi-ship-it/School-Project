Physical & Chemical Changes

Understanding Matter Transformations



















Physical Change










Definition :-

A physical change is a temporary change where a substance's physical properties like shape, size, or state of matter change, but its chemical composition remains the same and no new substance is formed.














Properties of Physical Change








      → No New Substance Formed


      → Usually Reversible


      → Change in Physical Properties Only


      → Minimal Energy Change


      → Molecular Rearrangement












Chemical Change

Definition :-

A chemical change is a process that converts one or more substances into new substances with entirely different properties, involving the rearrangement of atoms and the breaking and forming of chemical bonds.


























Properties of Chemical Change








      → Formation of New Substance


      → Usually Reversible


      → Significant Energy Change


      → Changes in Chemical Properties

      → Atmoic Rearrangement










Key Differences



Physical Changes

No new substance formed. Shape,size,or state changes only.

Chemical Changes

New substance formed. Atoms rearrange to create different matter.




Physical Change Examples




  • Dissolving Salt in Water


  • Making Alloys


  • Sublimation of Iodine


  • Magnetizing and Demagnetizing Iron


























Chemical Change Examples




  • Cooking and burning sugar

  • Rusting vs simple corrosion of metals

  • Milk turning into curd

  • Burning a candle
